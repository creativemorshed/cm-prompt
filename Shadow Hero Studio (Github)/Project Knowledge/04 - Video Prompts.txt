Generate cinematic video prompts in English with clear motion instructions.

Video prompts are written in English because they are optimized for professional AI video generation tools such as Runway, Kling, Sora, and similar platforms that perform best with English input.

Every video prompt must begin with its Scene Number (e.g., "Scene 3 — ") to maintain traceability to the approved script.

Every video prompt must include:

- Scene Number Reference
- Character Appearance — cross-referenced exactly from the Character Sheet (physical description, facial features, skin tone, hair, eye shape)
- Clothing — cross-referenced exactly from the Character Sheet (Clothing Style field: fabric, color, fit, accessories)
- Character Actions
- Subject Movement
- Environmental Motion
- Camera Direction (Shot Type + Camera Movement — integrated directly inside this prompt)
- Scene Atmosphere
- Cinematic Style
- Estimated Clip Duration (e.g., "Estimated clip duration: 8 to 12 seconds") — Default duration is 8 seconds unless the scene requires more.

Camera direction must be embedded directly inside each video prompt using cinematic language. Never generate Camera Direction as a separate output section.

Maintain consistency with the approved script and Character Sheets.

---

AVAILABLE SHOT TYPES:

- Wide Shot — establishes location, scale, isolation
- Medium Shot — natural conversation, character presence
- Close-Up — emotion, tension, reaction
- Extreme Close-Up — micro-detail, psychological intensity
- Over-the-Shoulder — perspective, confrontation, intimacy
- Tracking Shot — following movement, momentum, urgency
- Dolly Shot — gradual revelation, emotional pull-in or pull-out
- Crane Shot — grand scale, dramatic reveal, God's-eye perspective

---

AVAILABLE CAMERA MOVEMENTS:

- Static — stillness, weight, tension
- Push In — growing intensity, focus
- Pull Out — isolation, revelation of scale
- Pan — surveying, following, transition
- Tilt Up/Down — power, vulnerability, scale
- Handheld — urgency, chaos, intimacy
- Dolly Zoom — psychological distortion, dread

---

SELECTION RULE:
Always choose the shot type based on the emotional objective of the scene as defined in the Scene Breakdown. Never use a shot type without a clear emotional or narrative reason.

Avoid random or unmotivated camera movements.
