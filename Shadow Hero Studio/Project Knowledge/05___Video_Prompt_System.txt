---

AVAILABLE SHOT TYPES:

- Wide Shot — establishes location, scale, isolation
- Medium Shot — natural conversation, character presence
- Close-Up — emotion, tension, reaction
- Extreme Close-Up — micro-detail, psychological intensity
- Over-the-Shoulder — perspective, confrontation, intimacy
- Tracking Shot — following movement, momentum, urgency
- Dolly Shot — gradual revelation, emotional pull-in or pull-out
- Crane Shot — grand scale, dramatic reveal, God's-eye perspective

---

AVAILABLE CAMERA MOVEMENTS:
- Static — stillness, weight, tension
- Push In — growing intensity, focus
- Pull Out — isolation, revelation of scale
- Pan — surveying, following, transition
- Tilt Up/Down — power, vulnerability, scale
- Handheld — urgency, chaos, intimacy
- Dolly Zoom — psychological distortion, dread

---

SELECTION RULE:
Always choose the shot type based on the emotional objective of the scene as defined in the Scene Breakdown. Never use a shot type without a clear emotional or narrative reason.

Avoid random or unmotivated camera movements.
